# Frontend Design

Use this skill to make design decisions before changing code.

## Design Read

Identify:

- page type and primary goal;
- audience and user need;
- brand register: utilitarian, premium, playful, editorial, technical, calm, bold, or experimental;
- content truth: what claims are supported and what must not be invented;
- constraints: existing stack, routes, design system, assets, SEO, accessibility, and product flows;
- visual risk: generic gradients, fake proof, card-grid monotony, weak contrast, stock-like media, or marketing treatment on product UI.

## Direction

Choose a specific design direction with acceptance criteria. Keep it concrete: density, spacing, typography, imagery, rhythm, interaction, and proof points.

For dashboards, tools, CRMs, admin panels, and operational UIs, prioritize scanability, hierarchy, repeated-use efficiency, clear states, and restrained styling. Do not force landing-page hero logic onto product UI.

For landing pages, make the offer or product clear in the first viewport. Use truthful proof only. Avoid fake metrics, fake customer logos, generic CTAs, and decorative sections that do not advance the page goal.

## Checks

- Does the page communicate the actual product or task quickly?
- Does the visual system have contrast, rhythm, and hierarchy?
- Are repeated sections varied by purpose, not just by background color?
- Are media and illustrations specific enough to be useful?
- Is motion used to clarify or enrich the design rather than distract?
